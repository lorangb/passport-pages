Inside the Book
\uD83C\uDFF0

The Tower of London Used to Be a Zoo

For over 600 years, the Tower housed lions, polar bears, and even an elephant! The Royal Menagerie was one of London's biggest attractions.

\uD83D\uDD73\uFE0F

Big Ben Is Not the Clock Tower

"Big Ben" is the nickname of the 13.7-ton bell inside. The tower itself is called the Elizabeth Tower. And the clock face? Nearly 23 feet across!

\uD83D\uDC7B

The Underground's Ghost Stations

Over 40 abandoned Tube stations hide beneath London's streets. Aldwych station was used to store priceless art during WWII to keep it safe from bombs.

\uD83C\uDF6B

Fish and Chips Saved the Nation

During both World Wars, fish and chips were among the only foods not rationed. The government knew a happy nation needed its comfort food!

The Story

Born from one family trip and a 12-year-old fact-lover.

It started with a trip to the UK. Our son Greyson (12) devours fact books like they're candy \u2014 Nat\/Geo, Usborne, world records, philosophy, money, all of it. Our daughter Sophie (9) wants stories and beauty on every page.

We couldn't find anything that made our actual destinations come alive for them. So we built it ourselves \u2014 starting with our own trip to Brighton, the Cotswolds, and London. Then we realized: every family deserves this for their trip.

Passport Pages was born. One book per destination. Three age editions. Every language we can manage. Beautiful enough to keep forever, practical enough for the plane ride.
